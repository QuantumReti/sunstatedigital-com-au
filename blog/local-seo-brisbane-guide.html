Local SEO Brisbane: The Complete Guide for Small Business Owners (2026)

Local SEO is the highest-ROI marketing channel available to a small Brisbane business. Free, compounding, and 24/7. This is the complete guide — no jargon, just what works.

Local SEO is the art and science of getting your Brisbane business to appear when people in your area search for what you sell. Done well, it's the highest-ROI marketing channel available to a small business — free, compounding, and 24/7. This is the complete guide, without the jargon.

What Is Local SEO and Why Does It Matter?

When someone in Chermside searches "dentist near me" or "best cafe Fortitude Valley," Google shows them results ranked by local relevance. Local SEO is how you tell Google that your business is the most relevant, trustworthy option for that search.

The difference between ranking #1 and #4 locally isn't just pride — it's revenue. The top 3 results in Google's local "map pack" capture over 75% of all clicks. Position 4 and below share the remaining 25%. Local SEO is fundamentally about getting into that top 3.

Step 1: Set Up and Optimise Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the foundation of local SEO. Without a complete, optimised profile, you're leaving your ranking to chance.

Here's your GBP checklist:

  • Business name: Use your exact trading name — don't stuff keywords into your business name
  • Primary category: Choose the most specific, relevant category (e.g., "Physiotherapist" not "Health Clinic")
  • Secondary categories: Add all relevant service categories
  • Address and service area: Add your physical address AND the suburbs you service
  • Hours: Keep these accurate and update them for public holidays
  • Phone and website: Must be consistent with every other listing online
  • Photos: Upload 10+ high-quality photos. Google rewards active profiles
  • Products/Services: Describe each service with relevant keywords and pricing
  • Weekly posts: Post updates, offers, or tips at least once a week

Step 2: Build Your Google Review Strategy

Reviews are the #1 local ranking factor that business owners have direct control over. A business with 80+ reviews at 4.7 stars will outrank a competitor with 10 reviews at 4.9 stars — volume and recency both matter.

Getting Reviews Consistently

  • Ask every happy customer immediately after service
  • Create a short URL that goes directly to your Google review form
  • Send a follow-up SMS or email with the link (our AI Jetpack automates this entirely)
  • Make it a habit — one new review per week is 52 reviews per year

Never buy reviews or ask for reviews in exchange for discounts. Google detects and penalises this. Earn them genuinely — it's not hard if you deliver good service.

Step 3: On-Page SEO for Your Website

Your website needs to clearly tell Google what you do and where you do it. This sounds simple, but most Brisbane business websites get it wrong.

Every page on your website should have:

  • A clear title tag that includes your main service and location (e.g., "Plumber Brisbane Northside | XYZ Plumbing")
  • A meta description that's compelling and includes your key terms
  • H1 and H2 headings that mention your services and suburbs
  • Location mentions throughout the content — not stuffed, but natural
  • Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on every page
  • Schema markup — structured data that helps Google understand your business type and location

Step 4: Local Citations — Getting Listed Everywhere That Matters

A local citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number. Citations from authoritative directories act as trust signals for Google — they confirm that your business is legitimate and located where you say it is.

Priority citations for Brisbane businesses:

  • Google Business Profile (already covered)
  • Apple Maps
  • Bing Places
  • True Local
  • Yellow Pages Australia
  • Hotfrog
  • Industry-specific directories (e.g., Hipages for tradies, HealthEngine for health businesses)
  • Your local council's business directory

Critical rule: Your NAP must be identical across every listing. "St" vs "Street" can create inconsistencies that hurt your ranking.

Step 5: Suburb-Specific Landing Pages

If you serve multiple Brisbane suburbs, create a dedicated landing page for each key suburb. A plumber who serves Brisbane, Ipswich, and Logan should have separate pages for each.

Each suburb page should:

  • Have unique content specific to that suburb (not just copy-paste with the suburb name changed)
  • Mention local landmarks, streets, or areas in a natural way
  • Include suburb-specific testimonials if possible
  • Have its own meta title and description targeting that suburb

How Long Does Local SEO Take to Work?

Honest answer: 3–6 months for meaningful results, 6–12 months for significant movement on competitive terms.

This isn't a knock against SEO — it's just how Google works. The algorithm needs time to see consistent signals: your GBP activity, your reviews accumulating, your citations being built, your website being crawled and indexed.

What you will see faster:

  • Your GBP ranking improving within 4–8 weeks of proper optimisation
  • Local map pack appearances for low-competition suburb terms within 2–3 months
  • Organic search traffic growing steadily over the first 6 months

Realistic Expectations — What Good SEO Actually Looks Like

A cafe in West End won't rank #1 for "cafe Brisbane" in three months — that's an intensely competitive term. But they can rank #1 for "cafe West End" in 2–3 months, and for "brunch West End Brisbane" shortly after. Specific, local, long-tail terms are where small businesses win — and those terms convert better because the searcher's intent is very specific.
