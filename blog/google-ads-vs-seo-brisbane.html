Google Ads vs SEO for Brisbane Businesses: Which One Should You Choose?

Google Ads gives you instant traffic. SEO builds you long-term rankings. The question isn't which one is better — it's which one your business needs right now. Here's the honest answer.

This is one of the most common questions we get from Brisbane business owners: "Should I do Google Ads or SEO?" It's a great question, and the honest answer is — it depends on where your business is right now. Let's break down both channels properly, without the spin.

What Is Local SEO?

SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) is the process of improving your website and online presence so that Google ranks you higher in organic (unpaid) search results. When someone searches "electrician Brisbane Northside" and you appear in the results without paying for that click — that's SEO working.

The upside: Once you rank, the traffic is free. A business ranking #1 for a high-volume local term can receive hundreds of visitors per month at zero ongoing cost. The ROI of good SEO, compounded over 2–3 years, is extraordinary.

The downside: It takes time. Typically 3–6 months to see meaningful results, and 6–12 months to rank for competitive terms. If your business needs leads in the next 30 days, SEO alone won't cut it.

What Are Google Ads?

Google Ads (also called Pay-Per-Click or PPC) lets you pay to appear at the top of search results instantly. You choose your keywords, set a budget, and pay each time someone clicks your ad. Turn it on today and you can have leads tomorrow.

The upside: Speed. Immediate visibility. The ability to test messaging and offers quickly. Precise control over budget, targeting, and keywords. Perfect for new businesses, seasonal promotions, or testing a new service.

The downside: You pay for every click, and clicks in competitive industries can be expensive. A plumber in Brisbane might pay $15–40 per click. Stop paying and the traffic stops immediately. There's no compounding benefit — you're renting visibility, not owning it.

Brisbane Industry Benchmarks: What Do Clicks Actually Cost?

  • Plumbers / Electricians / Tradies: $8–35 per click
  • Lawyers / Financial Services: $20–60 per click
  • Dentists / Health Clinics: $10–30 per click
  • Cafes / Restaurants: $1–5 per click
  • eCommerce / Retail: $1–8 per click

High cost-per-click doesn't mean Google Ads isn't worth it — it means you need strong conversion on your landing page to make the economics work. A plumber spending $30/click but converting at 15% (1 in 7 clicks becomes an enquiry) and closing 50% of enquiries is spending about $400 to acquire a customer. If average job value is $800+, that's excellent ROI.

SEO vs Google Ads: A Direct Comparison

Time to results: Google Ads — 24–48 hours. SEO — 3–12 months.

Cost structure: Google Ads — ongoing spend, pay per click. SEO — upfront work cost, free traffic long-term.

Scalability: Google Ads — instant, increase budget anytime. SEO — slow to scale but exponentially valuable.

Reliability: Google Ads — consistent while budget is live. SEO — can fluctuate with algorithm updates.

Long-term ROI: Google Ads — good if managed well. SEO — exceptional if done right.

Best for: Google Ads — new businesses, seasonal campaigns, fast-moving offers. SEO — established businesses, long-term growth, reducing ad spend over time.

The Real Answer: Start With Ads, Build SEO Alongside

For most Brisbane businesses, the ideal strategy isn't either/or — it's both, in sequence.

Here's the playbook we recommend:

  1. Month 1–3: Launch Google Ads to start generating leads immediately. Test different messages, offers, and landing pages. Find what converts
  2. Month 1 onwards: Start SEO simultaneously — GBP optimisation, website fixes, review building. The early work takes time to compound
  3. Month 6–12: SEO starts delivering consistent organic traffic. Reduce Google Ads spend if ROI is lower than organic
  4. Year 2+: SEO is your primary lead source. Google Ads is used tactically — for new services, seasonal promotions, or competitive terms you haven't ranked for yet

This approach means you're never waiting 6 months for your first lead, but you're also building a sustainable, compounding asset that doesn't require ongoing ad spend.
