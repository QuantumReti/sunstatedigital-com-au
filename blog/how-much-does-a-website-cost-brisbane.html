How Much Does a Website Cost in Brisbane? (2026 Honest Guide)

Websites in Brisbane can cost $0 or $15,000. The real question isn't what it costs — it's what it's worth. Here's the honest breakdown, including exactly where we fit and why.

If you've been Googling "how much does a website cost Brisbane," you've probably found answers ranging from $0 to $50,000. That's not helpful. Let's cut through the noise and give you the honest answer — including where Sun State Digital sits in the market and why.

Option 1: DIY (Squarespace / Wix) — $20–50/month

These platforms are marketed as simple, affordable, and anyone-can-do-it. And technically, that's true. You can build something that looks like a website in a weekend.

But here's what they don't tell you:

  • The time cost is real. Most Brisbane business owners spend 15–30 hours building their first DIY website, then more time every time something needs updating
  • The SEO is weak out of the box. DIY websites rarely rank well on Google without significant technical knowledge
  • The design ceiling is low. Templates look like templates. Customers notice, and it affects your credibility
  • Hidden costs add up. Custom domains, premium features, payment processing, email — it quickly gets to $60–80/month

DIY works if you have time, some technical aptitude, and a very simple business. For most Brisbane business owners, the opportunity cost of your time makes it the most expensive option of all.

Option 2: Freelancer — $500–3,000

Hiring a freelancer from Airtasker, Upwork, or Facebook groups is the most unpredictable option. You might get someone great — or someone who takes your deposit, disappears for six weeks, and delivers something embarrassing.

The range is huge because quality is wildly inconsistent. A $500 freelancer in Brisbane might be a graphic design student with no SEO knowledge. A $3,000 freelancer might be excellent but booked out for months.

Biggest risks with freelancers:

  • No accountability if something goes wrong
  • Limited ongoing support — you're often on your own after handover
  • SEO and mobile optimisation are often afterthoughts
  • Can take 4–8 weeks for delivery

Option 3: Digital Agency — $3,000–15,000+

A proper Brisbane digital agency will give you a strategic, polished website. They'll have a designer, a developer, an SEO specialist, and a project manager — and that's exactly why it costs what it costs.

For large businesses with complex needs, enterprise ecommerce, or significant marketing budgets, an agency is absolutely the right choice. But for most small to medium Brisbane businesses — a cafe, a tradie, a health practice, a retail store — paying $8,000 for a website is overkill. You'll spend years waiting to recoup that investment.
