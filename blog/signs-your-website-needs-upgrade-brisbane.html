5 Signs Your Brisbane Business Website Is Costing You Customers

Most failing websites don't announce themselves. They just quietly lose enquiries, one by one, while you wonder why the phone isn't ringing. Here are the five warning signs to look for.

Your website might be costing you customers right now — and you wouldn't even know it. Unlike a broken shopfront or a disconnected phone line, a failing website just quietly leaks opportunities. Here are the five warning signs that your Brisbane business website needs an urgent upgrade.

Sign #1: It's Not Mobile-Friendly

More than 60% of all local searches happen on a smartphone. In Brisbane specifically, that number climbs even higher for service-based businesses — people searching for tradies, restaurants, health services, and retail are overwhelmingly doing it on their phone.

If your website isn't designed for mobile — if text is too small to read, buttons are too close together to tap, or you have to pinch-and-zoom to see anything — you're losing more than half your potential customers before they even read a word.

The fix: A mobile-first website where every element is designed for a phone screen first, desktop second. Every website we build at Sun State Digital is mobile-first by default, and we test on real devices before launch.

How to test yours: Open your website on your own phone right now. If it's frustrating to use, your customers feel the same way.

Sign #2: It Loads Slowly

Google has published its own research on this: 50% of mobile users abandon a website that takes more than 3 seconds to load. Three seconds. That's not a long time — and yet most outdated Brisbane business websites take 5, 8, even 12 seconds to load on mobile.

Speed affects two things: user experience (people leave before they even see your offer) and Google ranking (slow websites rank lower, full stop). Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking factor, and a slow website actively hurts your SEO.

Common culprits for slow loading:

  • Unoptimised images (a 5MB photo where a 200KB photo would do)
  • Cheap shared hosting that can't handle load
  • Outdated website platforms with bloated code
  • Too many plugins or third-party scripts

The fix: A modern, lean website on fast hosting. Our websites load in under 2 seconds on mobile because we build them clean and host them on enterprise-grade infrastructure.

Sign #3: There's No Clear Call-to-Action

Ask yourself this: within 5 seconds of landing on your homepage, does a stranger know exactly what you do, who you serve, and what they should do next?

If your website is a wall of text about your company history with a small "Contact Us" link buried in the footer, you have a call-to-action problem. Visitors don't explore — they scan, decide quickly, and either act or leave.

A clear call-to-action means:

  • A large, prominent phone number at the top of every page
  • A clear primary action — "Book a Free Quote," "Call Now," "Get Started"
  • That action repeated multiple times as you scroll down the page
  • A contact form that's short, simple, and actually works

The fix: Restructure your homepage with a clear value proposition ("Brisbane's Fastest Electrician — Live in 24 Hours") and an unmissable action button. Don't make visitors work to contact you.

Sign #4: Google Can't Find You

You can have the most beautiful website in Brisbane, but if Google doesn't rank it, it might as well not exist. A website with no SEO is like a billboard on a road with no traffic.

Warning signs that Google can't find you:

  • Search your business name on Google — do you appear? If not, you're in serious trouble
  • Search "[your service] [your suburb]" — are you on the first page? Third page? Not there at all?
  • Check your Google Search Console (if you've set it up) — how many clicks per month is Google sending you?
  • Your website has no meta titles, meta descriptions, or heading structure

Basic SEO on a website is non-negotiable in 2026. Every page needs a title, a description, proper heading structure, and location references. Without these, you're invisible.

The fix: At minimum, ensure every page has proper meta tags, your Google Business Profile is linked to your website, and your site is submitted to Google Search Console. Better yet, invest in proper on-page SEO or a monthly SEO service to build your rankings over time.

Sign #5: It Looks Like It Was Built in 2015

Design trends move fast, and an outdated website sends a powerful subconscious message to potential customers: this business doesn't invest in itself. If they can't be bothered updating their website, what does that say about how they'll treat me as a customer?

Signs your website looks old:

  • Generic stock photos of people shaking hands in offices
  • Cluttered layouts with too much text and too many elements competing for attention
  • Dated fonts (Comic Sans, Impact, or web fonts from 2010)
  • No SSL certificate (your URL shows "Not Secure" in the browser)
  • It looks different on every device
  • Flash elements, auto-playing music, or pop-up ads

The fix: A fresh website built on modern design principles — clean, fast, mobile-first, with a clear visual hierarchy and genuine photos of your business and work.
